Washing Machine Problems We Fix in Fishers

  • Washer won't drain: Standing water in the drum after a cycle points to a clogged pump filter, blocked drain hose, or failed drain pump. We identify the root cause and fix it, not just clear the symptom.
  • Drum not spinning: A broken drive belt, worn motor coupling, or bad lid switch (top-loaders) prevents proper spinning. We carry belts and couplings for most brands.
  • Washer is leaking: Leaks from the front, sides, or bottom of the machine need prompt attention to prevent water damage. Common causes include a torn door gasket, loose hose, or cracked pump housing.
  • Machine won't start: A faulty door latch, failed thermal fuse, control board error, or broken start switch can prevent the washer from powering on.
  • Excessive vibration or noise: Worn shock absorbers, damaged drum bearings, or objects caught in the drum create disruptive noise and vibration that accelerates further wear.
  • Washer not filling with water: A malfunctioning water inlet valve or pressure switch can prevent proper water entry, resulting in partial or failed cycles.
  • Error codes on display: Modern washers from LG, Samsung, and Whirlpool display specific error codes. We understand what each code means and use it to guide a precise diagnosis.
  • Detergent not dispensing: A clogged or broken detergent drawer can prevent soap from entering the cycle, resulting in dirty laundry even with a full cycle completed.

Front-Load vs Top-Load Washer Repair in Fishers, Indiana

Front-load washers are common in newer Fishers homes and offer excellent efficiency and cleaning performance. They're prone to door seal deterioration, bearing noise, drum spider arm fatigue, and mold buildup around the door gasket. Electronic control board failures are also a known issue in some models. We address all of these with genuine or high-quality compatible parts.

Top-load washers - both traditional agitator and modern high-efficiency models - have their own service requirements. Agitator dog wear, lid switch failure, transmission problems, and pump issues are the most common repairs we perform on top-loaders in Fishers.
