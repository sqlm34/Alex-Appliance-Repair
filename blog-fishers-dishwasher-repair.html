Common Dishwasher Problems We Fix in Fishers

Our Fishers technicians have diagnosed and repaired every type of dishwasher failure. Here are the most frequent issues we handle:

Not draining

Standing water after a cycle indicates a clogged drain filter, blocked hose, or failed drain pump.

Dishes not getting clean

Clogged spray arm holes, a dirty filter, or a failing wash motor result in poor cleaning performance.

Dishwasher leaking

Water pooling under the appliance suggests a damaged door gasket, loose hose, or cracked pump housing. Leaks can damage flooring — address them promptly.

Won't start

A faulty door latch, blown thermal fuse, or defective control board can prevent the dishwasher from running.

Dishes still wet after drying

A failed heating element or broken rinse aid dispenser results in wet dishes at cycle end.

Making loud noise

Grinding or humming sounds usually point to a worn wash pump or debris inside the pump.

Bad odor

Food debris trapped in the filter or drain creates persistent smells. We clean internal components and fix drainage problems.

Door not latching

A broken door latch or damaged hinge prevents the dishwasher from starting. We repair or replace the latch assembly.
