Freezer Repair Services

Cooling and frost issues fixed

For some households, a freezer is not just a convenience appliance. It is essential infrastructure. Maybe you buy in bulk, meal prep on Sundays, store fish or game, or just rely on backup food options.

Then the freezer stops maintaining temperature, and you are watching your food investment melt away. A broken freezer is not a minor inconvenience. It is a time-sensitive problem that needs attention quickly.

Why Freezers Stop Freezing Properly

Freezer problems usually come from the cooling system, airflow, defrost system, thermostat, or dirty coils.

  • The compressor is struggling. The compressor circulates refrigerant through the system. If it weakens, the freezer may run but never get cold enough.
  • The evaporator coils are iced over. Excessive frost usually means the defrost cycle is not working correctly or airflow is blocked.
  • The refrigerant charge is low. A leak can reduce cooling performance and requires proper leak detection, repair, and recharge.
  • The condenser coils are filthy. If these coils are clogged with dust, the freezer cannot release heat efficiently. Cleaning them can sometimes solve the problem entirely.
  • The thermostat is reading temperature incorrectly. A faulty thermostat may prevent the freezer from cycling properly.

The Defrost Cycle Issue

Many freezers automatically warm the coils just enough to melt frost, then drain the water away. If that system fails, frost builds up until the freezer stops working efficiently. The cause may be a defrost timer, heater element, sensor, or drain issue. These problems are usually repairable.

Old Freezers Behave Differently

Older standalone freezers are different from newer refrigerator-freezer combinations. Chest freezers also fail differently than upright freezers. Alex Appliance Repair works with chest freezers, upright freezers, and freezer compartments in refrigerator-freezer units.

What Our Service Looks Like

  1. We start with the symptom. Is the freezer not cold enough, building frost, leaking, or making unusual sounds?
  2. We inspect airflow and coils. We look for frost buildup, blocked coils, fan issues, and condenser problems.
  3. We check the defrost drain. Drain problems can create ice buildup and water issues.
  4. We test controls and components. We check thermostat function and electrical parts when needed.
  5. We explain the repair. You get the diagnosis, cost, and repair path before work begins.

Freezer Brands We Service

We service Frigidaire, GE, Whirlpool, Maytag, Electrolux, Kenmore, Bosch, LG, Samsung, and other freezer brands. We work on chest freezers, upright freezers, and refrigerator-freezer combinations where the freezer section is not working correctly.

Repair Versus Replacement Logic

A freezer less than 10 years old that needs a repair is often worth fixing. A freezer that is 15 years old or older with a failing compressor may be worth replacing, especially if efficiency is poor. Most freezer problems can be repaired without replacing the unit unless the freezer is very old or the sealed system repair is too costly.

Real Freezer Repair Scenarios

We have cleared blocked defrost drains that caused heavy frost, replaced failed thermostats that kept freezers too warm, cleaned condenser coils that restored normal cooling, and diagnosed defrost system failures in refrigerator-freezer units. Sometimes the repair is simple. Sometimes it is involved. The key is diagnosing it quickly.

The Food Loss Problem

When a freezer stops working, you are racing against time before food thaws and spoils. Call immediately if your freezer stops maintaining cold. If you can temporarily relocate frozen goods, do it, but do not wait and hope the freezer fixes itself.

Maintenance That Actually Helps

  • Keep condenser coils clean and free of dust.
  • Do not overstuff the freezer because air needs to circulate.
  • Vacuum around the freezer exterior periodically.
  • Make sure the door seal closes tightly.
  • Keep the defrost drain clear when the unit has automatic defrost.

The Temperature Question

A freezer should maintain around 0 degrees Fahrenheit. If ice cream gets soft, frozen foods develop heavy ice crystals, or the temperature keeps creeping up, something is wrong. Do not wait for it to become a bigger food-loss problem.
