Carmel homeowners know the value of keeping their home running smoothly. A malfunctioning dishwasher means handwashing, wasted water, and frustration. Our technicians arrive equipped with diagnostic tools and common replacement parts for all major brands, so we can resolve most dishwasher problems on the first visit.

Common Dishwasher Problems We Fix in Carmel

  • Dishwasher not draining: Standing water at the bottom of the tub after a cycle is usually caused by a clogged drain filter, blocked drain hose, or failed drain pump. We clear blockages and replace failed pumps quickly.
  • Dishes coming out dirty: If your dishwasher isn't cleaning properly, the spray arms may be clogged, the filter may be dirty, or the wash motor may be failing. We inspect all wash components and restore full cleaning performance.
  • Dishwasher leaking: Water pooling under the dishwasher indicates a damaged door gasket, loose hose connection, or cracked pump housing. Leaks can damage your flooring - don't delay this repair.
  • Dishwasher won't start: A faulty door latch, blown thermal fuse, or defective control board can prevent the dishwasher from running. We test each component systematically to find the cause.
  • Dishes still wet after cycle: A failed heating element or broken rinse aid dispenser results in wet dishes at the end of the cycle. We restore the drying function so your dishes come out ready to put away.
  • Dishwasher making loud noise: Grinding, humming, or banging sounds often point to a worn wash pump, debris in the pump, or a failing spray arm bearing.
  • Bad odors from the dishwasher: Food debris trapped in the filter or drain can create unpleasant smells. We clean the internal components and identify any drainage problems that are allowing odors to develop.
  • Door won't latch or close properly: A broken door latch or damaged hinge prevents the dishwasher from starting. We repair or replace the latch assembly to restore normal operation.

Dishwasher Repair vs Replacement in Carmel

One question our Carmel customers often ask is whether it's worth repairing their dishwasher or better to simply buy a new one. Here's the honest answer:

If your dishwasher is less than 10 years old and the repair cost is less than half the price of a comparable new model, repair is almost always the better investment. A new entry-level dishwasher costs $500 - $800, while most repairs range from $100 - $350. Even for mid-range Bosch or KitchenAid models that cost $900 - $1,200 new, a repair that extends the life by several years is excellent value.

If the machine is over 12 years old and has multiple failing components, replacement may make more sense. Our technician will give you an honest recommendation after the diagnosis - with no pressure either way.
