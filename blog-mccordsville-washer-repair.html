Common Washer Problems We Fix in McCordsville

Our McCordsville technicians have diagnosed and repaired every type of washing machine failure. Here are the most frequent issues we handle:

Won't drain

Standing water after a cycle points to a clogged pump filter, blocked drain hose, or failed drain pump. We identify the root cause and fix it — not just clear a symptom.

Drum won't spin

A broken drive belt, worn motor coupling, or faulty lid switch prevents proper spinning. We carry belts and couplings for most major brands.

Washer is leaking

Leaks from the front, sides, or beneath the machine can come from a torn door gasket, loose hose, or cracked pump housing. We trace the source and seal it.

Won't start

A faulty door latch, blown thermal fuse, or control board fault can stop your washer from powering on. We test all electrical and mechanical components systematically.

Excessive vibration or noise

Worn shock absorbers, damaged drum bearings, or unbalanced loads create disruptive movement that accelerates further wear.

Displaying error codes

Modern washers flag problems with codes. Our technicians understand the code libraries for LG, Samsung, Whirlpool, and other brands.

Not filling with water

A defective water inlet valve or malfunctioning pressure switch can prevent proper water entry.

Bad odor

Mold and mildew build up inside front-load washers. We clean the drum, door seal, and drainage path to eliminate odors.
