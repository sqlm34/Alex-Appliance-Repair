Cooktop repair:
Common Issues, Causes, and Maintenance Tips

Cooktops are an essential part of the modern kitchen, providing convenience and a sleek appearance. There are various types of cooktops, including gas, electric, and induction, each with its own characteristics. A malfunctioning cooktop can significantly disrupt daily cooking routines. In this article, we’ll look at the main issues with cooktops, their causes, and maintenance tips to help extend the appliance’s lifespan.

Main Causes of Cooktop Breakdowns

Cooktops can malfunction for various reasons, such as:

  • Natural wear and tear of components
  • Incorrect installation and connection
  • Voltage fluctuations (for electric and induction cooktops)
  • Contamination and liquid spills on the control elements
  • Improper use, such as using incompatible cookware on an induction cooktop

Following the manufacturer’s guidelines and timely maintenance can help avoid most problems.

Common Cooktop Issues

  1. The cooktop won’t turn on If the cooktop doesn’t respond when turned on, the issue could be with the power supply, a damaged cable, or a faulty touch panel. For gas models, check the gas supply and ignition system.
  2. Burners won’t heat This issue could be due to malfunctioning heating elements (in electric cooktops) or problems with the control system. In induction models, it may indicate the use of incompatible cookware.
  3. Uneven heating If burners heat unevenly or not quickly enough, this could indicate a faulty thermostat or temperature sensors. Issues with internal wiring or relays may also be a factor.
  4. Burners turn off automatically In induction models, this is often related to safety systems that activate during overheating or when incompatible cookware is used. In other cases, it could indicate sensor malfunctions or control system errors.
  5. Noise during operation Some noise is normal for induction cooktops, but excessive noise could indicate problems with the cooling fan or internal components.
  6. Control panel issues If the touch panel buttons don’t respond, it could be due to contamination, liquid spills, or a malfunctioning panel. Cleaning and checking the panel often solve the problem, but serious malfunctions may require a replacement.

When to Call a Specialist

Some issues require professional intervention, especially those involving electrical or internal components. Contact a technician if:

  • The cooktop won’t turn on or turns off during operation
  • Burners don’t heat or heat unevenly
  • The touch panel isn’t responding
  • The cooktop makes loud or unusual noises
  • There are sparks or a burning smell

Professional diagnosis and repair can prevent the problem from worsening and avoid further damage.

Cooktop Maintenance Tips

Following these simple recommendations can help extend the life of your cooktop and reduce the risk of malfunctions:

  1. Regularly clean the cooktop. Grease and food residue can affect the performance of burners and touch controls.
  2. Use appropriate cookware, especially for induction models. Avoid using cookware with uneven or damaged bottoms, as this can affect heating.
  3. Prevent liquid spills on the control panel. Spilled water or other liquids can cause short circuits.
  4. Monitor the condition of the cable and connections. Replace the cable if there are any bends or damage.
  5. Use a voltage stabilizer to protect against power surges and prevent damage to electronic components.

Conclusion

Cooktop repair requires specialized knowledge and skills, especially for electric and induction models. Following care and proper usage recommendations can prevent most issues and extend the life of the appliance.
