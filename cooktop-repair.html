Cooktop Repair Services Glass top and burner fixes

Cooktops are easy to take for granted until something goes wrong. Every day someone is boiling water, sauteing vegetables, simmering sauce, or heating oil for cooking. The cooktop is just there, doing its job and making cooking possible.

Then one element does not heat, the glass top cracks, or a gas burner will not light. Suddenly you are working around the problem instead of cooking normally. Most cooktop issues are repairable. You do not need a new cooktop in many cases. You need someone who knows how these systems work.

The Different Cooktop Types

Cooktops are not all the same, and the repair depends on the technology.

  • Smooth glass top cooktops. These may use electric resistance elements under the glass or induction heating. Electric elements can fail individually, while induction units need compatible cookware and more specialized diagnostics.
  • Traditional coil cooktops. These use exposed coils that glow red when heated. Coils can often be replaced individually.
  • Gas cooktops. These rely on ignition and gas flow. Problems usually involve igniters, burner ports, valves, or control issues.

The Element That Just Won't Heat

This is one of the most straightforward cooktop problems. One burner or element stops heating while the others work. On electric cooktops, the element or control may be the cause. On gas cooktops, it may be the igniter, a blocked burner, or a valve issue. We test the system before recommending a repair.

The Glass Top That's Cracked

A cracked glass cooktop is a safety issue and a functional problem. It may not distribute heat properly, and the crack can spread. Sometimes the glass surface needs replacement. Sometimes the repair path depends on where the crack is and how deep it goes. This is a call-for-service situation, not a keep-using-it situation.

The Burner That Won't Light

On gas cooktops, the igniter creates the spark that lights the gas. If it fails, gas may flow without ignition. After a few failed attempts, stop trying. Unlit gas should not build up in the kitchen. Usually the repair is an igniter replacement, burner cleaning, or gas valve diagnosis.

Control Board Issues

Some cooktops use electronic controls. If the control board fails, burners may not respond, may not turn on, or may not turn off correctly. Control board replacement can restore proper response when the board is the real cause.

Surface Damage and Buildup

Sometimes what looks like serious damage is mineral buildup, food residue, or surface staining. We can identify whether something is actually broken or whether the cooktop needs cleaning, surface treatment, or a small part repair.

What Happens During Our Service Visit

  1. We inspect the issue. We check which elements, burners, or controls are not working.
  2. We test the system. We determine whether the cause is the element, igniter, control system, sensor, or another component.
  3. We assess glass damage carefully. We determine whether the damage affects safety or function.
  4. We explain the repair. You get a clear diagnosis and price before work begins.
  5. We use matching parts. Replacement parts are selected for your cooktop model.

Cooktop Brands We Work On

We service GE, Whirlpool, Maytag, LG, Samsung, Electrolux, Bosch, Wolf, Miele, Viking, and other cooktop brands. We work with electric resistance, induction, and gas cooktops, including standard and higher-end models.

Repair Worth Pursuing

A glass-top cooktop less than 10 years old that needs an element replacement is usually worth fixing. A 15-year-old cooktop that needs several components may be worth replacing. Most cooktop repairs are straightforward enough that replacement does not make sense unless the unit is very old or heavily damaged.

Real Cooktop Situations We've Handled

We have replaced failed glass-top elements, repaired gas burners with bad igniters, diagnosed control boards that made burners behave erratically, and cleaned mineral buildup that looked like permanent damage. In many cases, the cooktop was much more repairable than the customer expected.

Prevention and Maintenance

  • Do not place cold items directly on a hot glass cooktop surface.
  • Clean gently and avoid abrasive scrubbers that scratch glass.
  • Keep gas burners clean and clear of debris.
  • Use compatible cookware on induction cooktops.
  • Address cracks, sparks, and gas ignition issues quickly.
