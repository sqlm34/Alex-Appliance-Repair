Refrigerator repair:
Features, Common Problems, and Maintenance Tips

A refrigerator is an essential household appliance that plays a vital role in food storage and everyday convenience. However, like any appliance, it can break down and require repairs. In this article, we’ll look at the most common malfunctions, when it’s time to call a specialist, and offer some maintenance tips.

Main Causes of Refrigerator Breakdowns

Modern refrigerators are complex devices that combine mechanical, electrical, and electronic components. The most common causes of malfunctions include: Part wear and tear; Voltage fluctuations; Improper use; Incorrect installation conditions. Breakdowns can occur in various parts of the refrigerator, from the compressor to the control board. Let's examine the most frequent types of issues.

Common Refrigerator Issues

  • Not Cooling or Poor Cooling. This problem can be due to refrigerant leakage, a compressor malfunction, or a clogged capillary system. In this case, it’s best to call a specialist for diagnostics.
  • Overcooling If the refrigerator overcools products, it may be due to a faulty thermostat or temperature sensors. Often, a replacement of the faulty part is required.
  • Noise or Vibration Excessive noise can indicate compressor issues or improper installation. Make sure the refrigerator is placed on a level surface and check that the compressor isn’t in contact with other parts.
  • Seal Problems A worn-out door seal can lead to increased compressor load and higher energy consumption. Seals can wear over time and may need replacement.
  • Water Leaks Water under the refrigerator often indicates a clogged drainage system. Cleaning the drainage system usually resolves the problem.

When to Call a Specialist

Not all issues can be resolved independently. A qualified technician with the necessary tools and experience can diagnose and fix even complex problems and offer maintenance recommendations. Professional help is especially important if:

  • The refrigerator makes unusual noises
  • The door seal is compromised
  • The compressor runs non-stop
  • There is a refrigerant leak

Maintenance and Care Tips for Refrigerators

To extend the lifespan of your refrigerator, it’s essential to follow a few simple care tips:

  • Regularly clean door seals to prevent loss of airtightness.
  • Check and clean the drainage system at least once every six months.
  • Avoid overloading the refrigerator, as it helps maintain even cooling.
  • Monitor room temperature — refrigerators operating in very warm conditions may wear out faster.
  • Use a voltage stabilizer to protect the appliance from power surges.

Conclusion

Refrigerator repair is a process that requires knowledge and skills, so in many cases, it’s best to entrust this work to specialists. However, by following maintenance tips, you can reduce the risk of breakdowns and extend the appliance's service life.
