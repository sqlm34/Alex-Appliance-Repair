Dryer repair:
Common Issues, Causes, and Maintenance Tips

Dryers are useful household appliances that help significantly reduce drying time, especially in limited spaces and high-humidity environments. However, like any appliance, dryers can break down and require repair and maintenance. In this article, we’ll discuss common dryer issues, when to call a professional, and provide maintenance tips to extend the appliance's lifespan.

Main Causes of Dryer Breakdowns

Modern dryers include mechanical and electronic components, and issues can arise for various reasons:

  • Natural wear and tear
  • Overloading the machine
  • Improper use
  • Clogged filters and ventilation systems
  • Voltage fluctuations

Most issues can be prevented by following the manufacturer’s guidelines and maintaining the appliance regularly.

Common Dryer Problems

  1. The machine won’t turn on If the dryer won’t start, the problem could be due to a faulty power button, power supply issues, a damaged control board, or wiring defects. First, check the power connection and the outlet’s functionality.
  2. The machine doesn’t dry clothes properly This problem may be related to a clogged lint filter, a faulty heating element, or temperature sensors. Regular filter cleaning helps avoid this issue, but if it persists, professional diagnostics may be necessary.
  3. Excessive noise or vibration If the dryer makes unusual noises or vibrates excessively, worn drum bearings or shock absorbers could be the cause. To prevent this issue, avoid overloading the machine and maintain balanced loads.
  4. Overheating Overheating may be due to a faulty thermostat, clogged ventilation system, or defective heating element. Overheating is dangerous as it can damage internal components and potentially cause a fire.
  5. Drum not spinning If the drum stops spinning, the issue could be with the drive belt, motor, or electric clutch. Replacing the belt and diagnosing the motor are tasks best left to a qualified technician.
  6. Water leaks In some dryer models with a condensation function, water leaks can occur due to a damaged condensation reservoir or clogged hoses. Inspecting and cleaning these components can help resolve the issue.

When to Call a Professional

Some dryer issues are difficult to fix on your own, especially those involving electrical or mechanical components. It is recommended to contact a qualified technician if:

  • The machine doesn’t turn on
  • Clothes remain damp after a complete cycle
  • The appliance makes unusual noises or vibrations
  • The drum doesn’t spin
  • Overheating or a burning smell is detected

A professional technician will conduct diagnostics, identify the cause of the issue, and repair it using original parts and proven methods.

Dryer Maintenance Tips

Following simple maintenance guidelines can help prevent breakdowns and extend the appliance's lifespan:

  1. Regularly clean the lint filter to prevent clogs in the ventilation system and improve the machine’s efficiency.
  2. Inspect and clean ventilation openings and hoses at least once every few months.
  3. Avoid overloading the dryer and follow the recommended load capacity.
  4. Monitor the room’s humidity and temperature where the machine is located to prevent excessive overheating.
  5. Use a voltage stabilizer to protect the appliance from power surges.

Conclusion

Repairing a dryer requires professional knowledge and experience, so for complex issues, it’s recommended to contact specialists. However, by following care and proper usage guidelines, you can prevent many issues and extend the life of your appliance.
