Stove and Oven Problems We Fix in Fishers

  • Gas burner won't ignite: A clogged burner port, cracked igniter, faulty spark module, or worn igniter wire are the most common causes. We clean, test, and replace components as needed.
  • Electric burner or bake element not heating: A burned-out surface element or bake element is a quick repair that restores full oven function. We carry elements for all standard sizes and brands.
  • Oven doesn't reach temperature: A failed temperature sensor, faulty control board, or failing bake element prevents the oven from reaching the set temperature. We test each component in sequence.
  • Oven heats unevenly: Hot spots and cold zones inside the oven indicate a failing element, poor broil element operation, or a malfunctioning convection fan in convection models.
  • Control panel won't respond: Electronic control boards in modern Fishers kitchen ranges can fail due to power surges or component wear. We test and replace the specific board or relay.
  • Gas oven won't stay lit: The oven ignites but the flame goes out after a few seconds - usually a faulty flame sensor or gas safety valve that isn't holding the valve open.
  • Self-cleaning cycle won't engage: A failed door lock motor or control board issue prevents the self-clean function from activating.
  • Stove top glass is cracked: A cracked ceramic glass cooktop surface is a safety concern. We assess the damage and advise on panel replacement options.

Gas Stove Repair in Fishers, Indiana

Gas ranges are favored by Fishers cooking enthusiasts for their instant heat and precise control. Our technicians are trained in gas appliance safety and handle all gas stove repairs - igniters, gas valves, flame sensors, regulators - with proper protocols. If you smell gas coming from your stove at any point, turn off the range, ventilate the kitchen, and call us immediately. Do not attempt to find the leak yourself.

Electric Stove and Oven Repair in Fishers, Indiana

Safety: When to Call Immediately for Stove Repair in Fishers

Some stove issues are inconvenient but not urgent. Others demand immediate attention. Call us right away - or your gas provider first - if you experience a persistent gas smell near the stove, sparking or arcing from the electrical system, smoke from behind the control panel, or any situation where the appliance cannot be safely shut off. These are not DIY situations.
