Dryer Repair Services Heating and tumble issues fixed

Here's a scenario we hear about constantly: someone throws a load of wet laundry into the dryer, hits the button, walks away expecting dry clothes in 45 minutes. An hour later, they open the door to find everything still soaking wet and barely warm.

That moment of "oh no" hits different when it comes to dryers. Some people try running the dryer again and again. Meanwhile their electric bill is climbing, and their clothes still are not dry. This is probably the most common complaint we hear, and usually it has nothing to do with the dryer being completely broken.

Why Your Dryer Takes Forever

Most dryer problems are fixable once the actual cause is identified. The issue may be airflow, heat, drum movement, or a worn mechanical part.

  • The vent line is clogged. This is the number one reason dryers do not work properly. When lint blocks the vent line, the dryer has to work harder to push moist air outside. Clothes take longer to dry, the dryer gets hotter, and energy use goes up.
  • The heating element is dying. On electric dryers, a weak heating element can leave you with lukewarm air even while the drum still turns. Replacing the element often brings the dryer back to normal performance.
  • The gas ignition system has failed. Gas dryers use an igniter, burner, thermostat, and gas valve system. If one part fails, the dryer may run with cold air. These repairs require the right tools and proper safety knowledge.
  • The drum rollers are worn out. Grinding, squealing, thumping, or dragging sounds often come from worn rollers. If ignored, the drum can strain other components and cause a larger repair.
  • The idler pulley is failing. This small part keeps tension on the drum belt. When it fails, the belt can slip, squeal, or stop turning the drum correctly.

What's Actually Worth Fixing

A dryer is usually worth repairing if it is less than 8 years old and the repair is under 50% of a replacement cost. Those machines often have plenty of life left. If a dryer is over 10 years old and needs several major parts, replacement may be the smarter move financially. We will tell you honestly which category your dryer falls into.

Gas vs Electric Dryer Repair

Electric dryers and gas dryers need different expertise. Electric dryers often involve heating elements, thermostats, sensors, motors, belts, and control boards. Gas dryers require knowledge of ignition systems, burner assemblies, gas valves, and safety protocols. Alex Appliance Repair works with both types and treats gas dryer problems with the caution they deserve.

The Vent Line Reality Check

A clogged dryer vent is not just an inconvenience. It is a fire risk. Lint is highly flammable, and when it builds up in your vent line, hot air moving past it can create a serious hazard. If your dryer has been taking longer than normal to dry clothes, the first thing to check is the vent. Professional dryer vent cleaning may be the only fix you need.

Real Scenarios From Our Workweek

We have repaired dryers that stopped heating because the heating element was nearly gone. We have fixed squealing dryers with a failing idler pulley in about 30 minutes. We have also cleaned vent lines with years of lint buildup and watched drying times return to normal immediately afterward.

In many cases, the customer expects to buy a new dryer, but the actual repair is much simpler and less expensive.

When You Should Call Us

If your dryer is running but not heating, making strange noises, taking too long to dry, leaving clothes damp, or giving off a burning smell, call Alex Appliance Repair. Do not try to fix gas dryer issues yourself. Gas lines and ignition systems are not something to experiment with.

The Maintenance Thing Nobody Does

Clean your lint trap after every load, but remember that the vent line still accumulates lint over time. Having it professionally cleaned every couple of years helps your dryer run efficiently, extends its lifespan, and keeps your home safer.
