Signs Your Washer Needs Repair

A malfunctioning washer can disrupt your routine and lead to costly water damage. Look out for these warning signs to avoid bigger issues.

  • Your washer won't start or stops mid-cycle.
  • Loud banging or excessive vibrations during operation.
  • Water leaks around or under the washer.
  • Clothes remain wet after a full spin cycle.
  • Unpleasant odors coming from the drum.
  • The washer isn't draining water properly.
  • Error codes appear on the control panel.
  • Detergent or water not dispensing correctly.

If you notice these problems, a professional washer repair company can diagnose and fix them quickly.

Preventive Maintenance Tips for Your Washer

Regular maintenance keeps your washer running smoothly and prevents costly repairs. Our washer repair experts recommend these simple care tips.

  • Clean the Drum & Detergent Tray - Residue buildup can cause odors and stains on clothes. Wipe the drum and detergent tray monthly to keep them fresh.
  • Inspect Hoses for Leaks - Cracked or loose hoses can lead to water damage. Check them regularly and replace if worn out.
  • Check Hoses Regularly - Cracked or loose hoses can cause leaks. Inspect and replace them every few years to prevent water damage.
  • Avoid Overloading the Washer - Too many clothes strain the motor and drum. Follow load size recommendations for efficient performance.
  • Use the Right Amount of Detergent - Excess detergent creates residue buildup inside the washer. Use only the recommended amount for your machine type.
  • Run a Monthly Cleaning Cycle - A hot water and vinegar cycle removes buildup and odors. This keeps your washer fresh and efficient.

For long-lasting performance, follow these tips along with timely servicing from a trusted washer repair expert.
